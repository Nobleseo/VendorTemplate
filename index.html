4 Things Missing From Most Vendor Posts

1
No reason to buy today. "Available for sale ₦12,000" is not enough. When there is no urgency, the customer tells themselves they will come back later — and later never comes.
2
No proof that others bought. Your customer is asking themselves — "has anyone else bought this? Is it real?" When you show them other happy buyers, their doubt goes away fast.

No clear instruction. People are lazy — in a good way. If you make them work to figure out how to buy, most of them will not bother. Tell them exactly what to do: "DM me the word HOLD right now." Simple.
4
No follow-up. This is where most vendors lose the most money. Someone was interested, they just got busy or forgot. One follow-up message from you would have closed that sale. But nobody sent it.

Your 5 Caption Templates

Copy. Fill in the [Gaps]. Post.

Everything in [square brackets] — just change it to your own product. That is all. Do not overthink it.

Quick instruction before you start: Post one caption per day — not all five at once. The best times to post are 7–9am or 6–9pm when people are most active on their phones. Rotate the captions every week. Never post the same one twice in a row or people will get used to it.

1
The Scarcity Caption
Works because: Fear of missing out
🧠 Why This One Works
The moment you say "last 3 remaining," something happens in the customer's brain — the product suddenly becomes more special than it was before. They do not want to miss out. This is the highest-converting caption for any physical product.
Last [3] pairs remaining 👟 [Name of your shoe/bag/item] — Sizes [38, 39, 41] are the only ones left. My supplier is out of stock. I won't get more until [next month]. Once these go, they go. Price: ₦[X] — payment before delivery. I deliver to [your city/area]. DM me your SIZE right now before someone else takes it 🙏
⏰ Post at 7am or 7pm 👜 Best for: Shoes, bags, clothing, anything with limited stock 📈 Expect: 3–8 DMs per post
💡 Pro tip Use a specific number — "last 3" works better than "limited stock." Specific feels more real. Even if you have 10, posting "last 5" creates more urgency. You are not lying — you are marketing.
2
The Happy Customer Caption
Works because: If she bought it and she's happy, maybe I should too
🧠 Why This One Works
Nigerian buyers do not want to be the first person to take a risk. When they see that someone else already bought and is pleased, their fear goes away. A real customer story sells your product better than the best product description ever will.
Customer just picked up her order this morning 😍 [Item name] in [colour] — she almost said no because of the price. Now see how she is feeling herself 😂 Three of her friends have already messaged me after seeing her with it. Same item still available — ₦[X] Sizes/colours: [list what you have] If she can look this good, so can you. DM me "I WANT" and I will send you full details 👇
⏰ Post at 12pm–2pm (lunch time) 👜 Best for: Fashion items, bags, beauty products 📈 Expect: 4–10 DMs — often the best-performing caption
💡 Pro tip Start collecting customer photos now. Even a blurry selfie in your item is gold. A real person photo on your WhatsApp status gets 5 times more attention than a studio photo. Ask every customer: "Please send me a photo when it arrives, I want to celebrate you."
3
The Quality Caption
Works because: She understands my frustration
🧠 Why This One Works
Most of your customers have been burnt before — they bought something cheap and it spoiled quickly. When you name that pain out loud and position your product as the solution, they feel understood. And people buy from people who understand them.
Are you tired of buying [shoes/bags] that [tear/fade/peel] after just 2 weeks? 😤 I was too. That's why I only stock [genuine leather / quality fabric / trusted brands]. My customers have been wearing and using their [item name] for [3–6 months] and it still looks brand new. I only sell what I would use myself. My name is my business. ₦[X] — [sizes/colours available] Delivery in [2–3 days] DM me "QUALITY" and I'll send you photos 📸✅
⏰ Post at 6–8pm (evening) 👜 Best for: Any product where quality matters 📈 Expect: Fewer DMs but serious, ready-to-pay buyers only
💡 Pro tip This caption brings in buyers who are willing to pay full price because they are tired of cheap products. Do NOT reduce your price for these people — they are actually judging your quality by how firm you hold your price.
4
The Half Payment Caption
Works because: The money barrier is cut in half
🧠 Why This One Works
Sometimes a customer wants your item but paying the full amount at once feels heavy. When you split it, the decision suddenly becomes much easier. This caption alone has made many vendors double their sales on days they used it. Use it carefully — only when you have the item ready and can deliver fast.
PAY HALF NOW. PAY HALF ON DELIVERY. 🙌 Yes, I'm doing this — but only for [this week / the next 5 customers]. [Item name] — Full price ₦[X] Pay ₦[half] now to reserve yours. Pay the remaining ₦[half] when I deliver to your door. Serious buyers only please 🙏 No time wasters. Sizes available: [list your sizes] Colours: [list your colours] DM me the word "HALF" right now to start your order ✅ Only [5] spots at this arrangement.
⏰ Post Monday–Wednesday morning 👜 Best for: Items priced ₦10,000 and above 📈 Usually the highest-converting caption in this whole pack
💡 Pro tip Only use this when you have the item in hand and can deliver within 48 hours. A failed half-payment experience will damage your reputation badly. But when you deliver on your promise, that customer will trust you forever and refer others.
5
The Bundle Deal Caption
Works because: I'm getting more for my money
🧠 Why This One Works
Nigerians love to feel like they are getting a deal — even a small saving makes people feel smart. Bundle offers also mean you sell more products per customer without needing more people. More money per sale. Same effort.
BUY 2, SAVE ₦[amount] 🎁 [Item 1] + [Item 2] — normally ₦[X] each Buy both today and pay only ₦[bundle price] That is ₦[saving] saved — goes straight back into your pocket. Perfect if you want to match your look 🔥 Or give one as a surprise gift for someone you love 🎀 Only [5] bundles at this price. After that it goes back to normal. DM me "BUNDLE" right now to claim yours 👇
⏰ Post Thursday–Friday (end of week treat energy) 👜 Best for: Matching sets, accessories, gifts 📈 Doubles your money per customer without needing more buyers
💡 Pro tip The bundle discount does not have to be big. ₦1,000 off feels like a win to a customer but still protects your profit. Never bundle items that are hard for you to replace — only use this with items you have enough stock of.

✅ The System That Closes Sales — Copy This
👩
How much for the bag?
👜
Hi! 👋 The bag is ₦15,500. I have it in black and brown. Delivery is free inside Lagos this week only! Which colour do you like so I can check if it's still available for you? 😊
— 24 hours pass with no reply —
👜
Hi! 👋 Just checking in — I still have the bag in both colours. Someone else was asking about the brown one this morning so I wanted to reach you first. I can hold it for 2 hours if you can confirm your colour right now 🙏
This single follow-up message recovers 30–40% of conversations you thought were dead. Use it.
When They Say "It's Too Expensive" — Here Is What to Say

Do not reduce immediately. Most vendors do this by reflex and they lose money every time. Try this first — it works on more than half of price complaints without you dropping your price at all:

Customer: "Abeg reduce small na. ₦15,500 too much." You: "Ha! I totally understand you 😊 Honestly this is already the lowest I can go for this quality — if I reduce it, it means I have to cut somewhere and that is usually the quality. And I don't do that to my customers. But since you are buying today, let me add [free delivery / a free dust bag / a small gift] for FREE on top. That is worth about ₦1,500 extra value, no charge. Which colour should I pack for you? 🎁"
💡 Why This Works You did not reduce the price — but the customer feels like they won something. The question "which colour should I pack?" at the end is very important. It moves them forward and assumes the sale is already happening. Most customers accept this offer and pay full price.
